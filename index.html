Magpies and Swooping in Australia


The Australian Magpie (Gymnorhina tibicen) is one of the most recognisable birds in Australia. They are extremely smart, capable of recognising up to 100 human faces, and are accomplished songbirds.
They are very common in Eastern Australia, and can be seen all over Victoria. Click on the months to see how sightings change over the year!


While magpies are extraordinary birds, they can be rather territorial at times. To protect their territory, they sometimes swoop at passers-by, which sometimes ends in injury. But what causes people to get swooped? Here's what some people were doing when they got swooped!
